About Me

I'm a computer science and mathematics student at Utah State University, working as a software engineer at the Space Dynamics Laboratory.

The GAS Team

I've had the opportunity to work on several projects through the Get Away Special (GAS) Team, a student-run organization at USU focused on small spacecraft and high-altitude balloon research.

ERRNO

ERRNO is a high-altitude balloon project that uses an Arduino Mega to collect atmospheric data during flight. The project gave me hands-on experience with embedded systems and sensor integration.

GASPACS

GASPACS is a 1U CubeSat project built around a Raspberry Pi Zero. The flight software is open source and the mission focused on deploying a small AeroBoom to demonstrate passive attitude control in low Earth orbit.

Cubium

Cubium is a C++ flight software framework originally written by Jack Miranda. I worked with this codebase to learn how small satellite software is structured, separating concerns between hardware abstraction, task scheduling, and inter-module communication.
